Some terms to know

Custom Model Horse:
Taking a plastic horse and changing it in anyway, such as repainting it.
Drastic Custom Model Horse:
Making drastic changes. Such as cutting off or fully removing parts of the horse to replace it either with parts from a different horse or sculpting new parts from clay. This process can involve the original model being chopped up into a dozen pieces before being put back together into something completly unrecognizable.
Artist Resin
Limited sculpts created by individual artists then cast and molded. Usually limited to 25-100 pieces. They tend to be quite expensive because you are paying for something really special. I've seen painted artist resins selling for up to 6 thousand dollars, while unpainted traditional sized ones hover around the 200-600 dollar mark. These are beautiful and unique pieces, always much higher quality then what companies produce.
Traditional:
Refers to horses around 12x9 inches large or 1/9 scale.
Classic:
Refers to horses around 9x6 inches large or 1/12 scale.
Stablemate:
Refers to horses aound 4x3 inches small or 1/32 scale.
Micro:
Refers to horses around 1 inch small or 1/64 scale.
